## F2 (HIGH) â€” The done-lane carrot was broadened to escape the no-DoD starvation, but on the actual needs-you cards the dominant CLEARING mechanism (a decision made -> the `needs-your-decision` tag removed via the override YAML) is the one the progress-guard explicitly EXCLUDES, so the one Phase-1 carrot still pays nothing on the most common real win

Why it fails the lens: round 4 my D4 said the carrot mostly cannot trigger because 11/18 programs have `dod.total:0`. Round 5's fix (good, I credited the broadening) counts ANY needs-you card leaving the set WITH a progress signal (`dod.met` up, `lane->done`, or `last_commit.iso` advanced). But then it correctly guards against false pays by EXCLUDING three non-finishes, one of which is "a tag/`blocked_on` removed by editing the override YAML". Here is the composition the plan did not run against its own data: a `needs-your-decision` card leaves needs-you precisely WHEN Mark makes the decision, and the way that shows up in `state.json` is the override YAML losing the `needs-your-decision` tag, which is the EXCLUDED case. The guard is correct (a YAML edit alone is not proof of work), but the consequence on this data is that the carrot's broadening did not actually un-starve it; it moved the starvation from "no DoD field" to "the close mechanism for the dominant card kind is the excluded one". The plan banks the carrot as Phase-1 motivational weight in R-8 and the 1.32 honest-scale list; on real data it is structurally near-silent for decisions and CADDC02-blocked items, which together are 4 of the 6 needs-you cards.

This is the same class as F1: a round-5 fix that is correct in isolation but, composed with how Mark's cards actually leave the board, delivers far less than the plan claims. And it matters more than F1 because the carrot is the ONE dedicated dopamine affordance the whole plan rests on (1.5: "the single carrot that adds a resting interaction affordance").

Minimal fix: no new mechanism; an honesty correction + one decision Mark signs off. (1) State plainly in 1.5 and R-8: "On current data the dominant needs-you clearing event is a decision made / a CADDC02 block lifted, which surfaces as a removed override tag (the excluded case), so the done-lane carrot will rarely fire in Phase 1; it pays only when a close coincides with a commit or a DoD tick. Stop counting it as Phase-1 motivational weight in 1.32 until the data can trigger it, the same honesty move round 5 already made for the goal-gradient (1.10). (2) The decision for Mark (add to open question 14, which already asks about the progress-guard): is a decision-made (a `needs-your-decision` tag clearing on a card that is NOT otherwise lapsing, e.g. its `time_sensitive` did not just pass) a finish that SHOULD pay a beat? There is a real argument it should: resolving a long-parked decision is the avoidance-loop close, the highest-value ADHD win on this board, and counting it would make the carrot fire on exactly the events that matter most. The risk is the YAML-edit could be a re-scope rather than a resolution. A middle option: count a `needs-your-decision` tag clearing as a finish ONLY when the card is NOT simultaneously a lapsing `time_sensitive` (so a deadline silently passing still pays nothing) AND `last_commit.iso` is within the last day (some work touched the repo recently), which separates "decided and worked" from "quietly deleted the tag". Either way, do not ship the carrot billed as Phase-1 weight while its guard excludes the way Mark's cards actually close.
